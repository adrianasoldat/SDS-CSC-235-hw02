Hypotheses & Insights

  • H1: Older adults have significantly higher COVID-19 hospitalization rates than younger age groups. The rate_by_age boxplot (which you have but didn't upload here) likely shows this, and it's strongly supported by existing literature. The age distribution chart confirms balanced representation across groups, so any rate differences are real, not an artifact of sample size.
  • H2: Hospitalization rates peaked in 2021–22 and have declined in subsequent seasons. Your season_distribution chart shows 2020–21 and 2021–22 had the most records, and year_distribution shows 2021 was the highest year. The rate histogram shows extreme right-skew driven by those peak years. This suggests a testable trend of declining severity over time.
  • H3: Geographic variation in hospitalization rates reflects differences in surveillance intensity rather than true disease burden. Your state_distribution chart shows Iowa, North Carolina, and Washington have dramatically fewer records than other states (~1,200, ~750, and ~200 vs. ~3,200 for most others). Yet the rate_by_state boxplot shows those same states have comparable or even higher median rates. This mismatch suggests reporting volume ≠ actual burden — a testable and interesting finding.
